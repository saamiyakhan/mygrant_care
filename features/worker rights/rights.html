Worker Rights

This section serves as a reminder to always keep your rights in mind and to stand up for yourself when necessary.

If you face any issues, you can raise them with MOM.

Refer to the main MOM website or use the direct forms below:

  • Report an Employment Act violation
  • Offences relating to pay, sick leave, etc.
  • Housing issues (safety/wellbeing)
  • Employment agency infringements
  • Suspected labour trafficking
  • Workplace safety or health lapses

Pay & Leave Rights

  • Construction workers are entitled to up to 60 days of paid hospitalisation leave annually (under the Employment Act).
  • Medical leave must be paid if certified by a doctor—whether for injury or illness—up to the statutory limit.
  • You may take up to 60 days of overseas leave per year (with levy waiver), if each trip is at least 7 days long.
  • Employers are required to retain your passport, work permit, and ticket copy during overseas leave.
