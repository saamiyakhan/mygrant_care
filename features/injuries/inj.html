Injury / Health Issues

This section guides you on steps to take and resources available if you're injured or facing health issues.

1. What do I do if I just got injured and am unable to work?
  • Contact your employer or manager immediately and seek medical assistance.
  • Under the Work Injury Compensation Act (WICA), you’re covered for up to $45,000 for outpatient and up to $60,000 for inpatient medical care.
  • You are typically entitled to 60 days of leave, but do confirm with your employer or legal aid.
2. What am I entitled to when I am injured or sick?
  • Paid sick leave if covered under the Employment Act (most employees are).
  • Number of days depends on your length of service with the employer.
  • Free medical consultations are available. Learn more via MOM or listed support centres.
3. I’m confused and need guidance on my health/employment issues.
  • Book a free consultation via this form or contact MWC Law Centre.
  • HealthServe also offers free help.
  • Check Ecosystem of Support for legal guidance.
4. I have a terminal illness or very high medical bills. What now?
  • Insurance may not cover everything — speak to your employer and legal advisors first.
  • Reach out to these organisations for help:
    • Criticare Fund for Migrant Workers
    • ItsRainingRaincoats
  • Let your family know about death compensation — see legal advisors.
5. My employer wants to fire me because I’m injured. Can they?
  • They legally cannot fire you while you're under medical treatment and still recovering.
  • They must provide food, shelter, and cover medical bills during recovery.
  • If wrongfully dismissed, consult the legal links here.
6. I feel lonely or stressed. Where can I get mental health help?
  • Book a free support session at HealthServe Mental Health & Counselling.
7. How can I keep myself safe and healthy?
  • Check this MOM resource on health and wellbeing.
