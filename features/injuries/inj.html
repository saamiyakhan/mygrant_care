Injury/ health issues

This section is to guide you on certain steps you can take and the resources available for your use in cases of injuries and health issues.

1. What do I do if I just got injured and am unable to work for a while?
  • Contact your employer/ manager immediately and inform them. Seek medical assistance! Do not be afraid of doing so, as you will be covered by medical insurance. If you got injured/sick in your workplace, under Work Injury Compensation Act (WICA), you will be insured for up to $45,000 for all your medical expenses, including in-patient and out-patient care. for up to $60,000 for inpatient medical care, where you are hospitalized. For how much leave you are allowed, it is trickier but typically you should have no issues getting leave for up to 60 days. However, check with your employer or some legal advisors linked here.
2. What am I entitled to when I am injured or sick?
    • You are entitled to sick leave. All employees, including Work Permit holders are entitled to paid sick leave, as long as you are covered under the Employment Act. (All employees under a contract of service with an employer are covered, but there are exceptions.)
    • The number of days of paid sick leave you are entitled to depends on how long you have worked under your employer for.

4. What if I have a terminal illness or very high bills exceeding my insurance coverage?
  • Insurance is not enough to cover your bills, terminal illnesses like late-stage cancer easily surpass the limit, and chemotherapy is outpatient care and usually not covered under insurance. When faced with such situations, talk to your employer, legal advisors linked in Legal Enquiries, and please reach out for support for your mental health linked in Mental Health. After speaking to your employers and legal advisors, you may have a better idea of your next steps. They might have shared with you non-profit organisations that raise funds for migrant workers. Some organisations you can approach are:
    • Criticare Fund for Migrant Workers, who specifically raise funds for the healthcare of migrant workers with terminal illnesses. This is the most suitable organisation for such cases.
    • ItsRainingRaincoats, an organisation built to help migrant workers, that might help you raise money too.
    Do be aware and inform your family of the compensation in cases of death. To get info about this, contact the legal advisors.
5. What do I do if I just got injured and am unable to work for a while?
  • Your employer generally cannot fire you for being sick or injured. They are legally obliged to cover you medical bills, provide you with (limited) paid leave, shelter and food. Until your situation resolves, they cannot fire you. If you feel like your employer is wrong in firing you, please approach the legal advisors linked here.
6. I feel lonely and stressed. Where and how can I get help?
  • Reach out for assistance and support by booking a slot at healthserve: Mental Health & Counselling Services - HealthServe.
