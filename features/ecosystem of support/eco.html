Ecosystem of Support and Resources

Medical/Dental care

1. Primary Care Plan (PCP) If you are under the primary care plan, you can use the FWMOMCare app to book appointments for medical care and purchase medication at subsidised rates. Pick your language and use the guide to install and set up the FWMOMCare app here. Watch this video for further guidance: How to see a doctor using FWMOMCare app (English). If unsure about whether you are under the PCP, contact your employer.
2. Free Vision Care by IRR Do you need glasses or have trouble seeing clearly? IRR’s eye doctors are here to help with any vision problems you may have. Eye check-ups and spectacles will be provided free of charge. Please sign up here.
3. Free Dental Care by IRR If you’re having minor dental problems like tooth pain, IRR is here to help. Their team can arrange a dental appointment for you, completely free of charge. Please sign up here.
4. Medical/Dental Care by Healthserve: Consultations and medication
  • $8 for acute condition
  • $15 for chronic condition
  • $15 for simple dental procedure
  • $30 for complex dental procedure
Check eligibility and appointment here.
5. SATA CommHealth Outreach resources. Here are some online resources regarding:
  • Stretching and strengthening exercises
  • Diets to manage blood pressure, cholestrol,and blood sugar levels.
  • Heat injury protection, heat stroke, managing heat stress, and heat stress protection
  • Common skin problems
Or you could refer to this link.

Legal Enquiries

1. MWC hotline for help: MWC operates a 24-hour helpline for migrant workers. The helpline is also made available in about 10 different languages native to migrant workers. Migrant workers in need of assistance on employment-related issues can call at +65 6536 2692.
2. Book consultation slot with MWC for help: Migrant workers who need legal help can visit the Migrant Workers’ Law Centre in MWC @ 579 Serangoon Road. Services provided for guidance and representation for the following:
  • Workplace Injuries
  • Employment or Salary disputes
  • Repatriation Concerns
  • Scams or money-lending problems
  • Criminal or other legal matters
    • To book an appointment, click here
3. FWMOMCare app: Use the app to contact a MOM officer by navigating to the ‘Talk to MOM’ section on the app’s menu or home page. From there, you can submit a request to speak with a MOM officer. If you do not have the app, you can pick your language and use the guide to install and set up the FWMOMCare app here.
4. HealthServe offers legal advice: Choose your preferred language and choose the rightmost button for help and advice from this link: Home EN – Healthserve.
MOM Helpline You can also contact the MOM helpline at 6438 5122 Phone operating hours Monday to Friday, 8:30AM to 5:30PM Saturday, 8:30AM to 1PM (Excluding public holidays)
